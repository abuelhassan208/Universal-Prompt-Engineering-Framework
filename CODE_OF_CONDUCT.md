# Code of Conduct

## ü§ù Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the Universal Prompt Engineering Framework community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## üìú Our Standards

### ‚úÖ Examples of Behavior That Contributes to a Positive Environment

- **Being Respectful**: Using welcoming and inclusive language
- **Being Collaborative**: Accepting constructive criticism gracefully
- **Being Empathetic**: Showing empathy towards other community members
- **Being Professional**: Focusing on what is best for the community
- **Being Supportive**: Helping newcomers and answering questions patiently
- **Being Ethical**: Following ethical AI principles and responsible use
- **Being Honest**: Giving credit where credit is due
- **Being Open-Minded**: Welcoming diverse perspectives and ideas

### ‚ùå Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

- **Harassment**: Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, personal or political attacks
- **Discrimination**: Discriminatory jokes, language, or behavior
- **Privacy Violations**: Publishing others' private information without permission
- **Inappropriate Content**: Sexual language, imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention
- **Disruption**: Sustained disruption of discussions or events
- **Spam**: Excessive self-promotion or off-topic content
- **Plagiarism**: Claiming others' work as your own
- **Unethical Use**: Using the framework for harmful, illegal, or unethical purposes
- **Bad Faith**: Deliberately providing misleading information or sabotaging the project

## ‚öñÔ∏è Enforcement Guidelines

Project maintainers will follow these Community Impact Guidelines:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other unprofessional behavior.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved for a specified period. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces and external channels. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period. No public or private interaction with the people involved is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment, or aggression.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any public interaction within the community.

## üõ°Ô∏è Ethical AI Use

### Responsible Use Principles

When using the Universal Prompt Engineering Framework:

1. **Do No Harm**: Do not use for harmful, illegal, or unethical purposes
2. **Respect Privacy**: Do not generate prompts that violate privacy
3. **Avoid Bias**: Be aware of and mitigate potential biases
4. **Be Transparent**: Disclose AI-generated content when appropriate
5. **Follow Laws**: Comply with all applicable laws and regulations
6. **Respect Rights**: Respect intellectual property and attribution
7. **Safety First**: Prioritize safety in all applications

### Prohibited Uses

The framework must NOT be used for:

- ‚ùå Generating harmful, illegal, or unethical content
- ‚ùå Creating misleading or deceptive information
- ‚ùå Violating privacy or data protection laws
- ‚ùå Harassment, bullying, or discrimination
- ‚ùå Unauthorized access or security breaches
- ‚ùå Plagiarism or copyright infringement
- ‚ùå Spam or malicious activities
- ‚ùå Any activity that violates applicable laws
